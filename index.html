Alamat ng Gubat

A Literary Work by Bob Ong

Introduction

Bob Ong is a Filipino writer and author known for his controversial writing techniques. Throughout his work, the problems and issues of the country are shown by reflecting on the Philippines' life. Ong pursued his passion for writing after dropping out of college. Bob Ong is not his real name. It is a pseudonym that he made when he was working as web designer and teacher.

Bob Ong has published numerous books, six of them having sold millions of copies. Aside from Alamat ng Gubat, his other well-known stories are "ABNKKBSNPLAKo", "Kapitan Sino", "Si", "Lumayo Ka Nga Sa Akin", and other works.

Alamat ng Gubat is the fourth book published by Bob Ong in 2003. The story is written as a humorous and straightforward narrative, depicting Filipinos' everyday lives. However, within the humorous story lies a message about the issues of our country.

Summary

In the story, Tong, a crab, goes on a trip to find a banana heart for his sick father. At first, he meets Buwaya and Maya, who cons Tong out of his pearls when he asks questions about the location of the heart. Buwaya attempts to eat Tong when he runs out of pearls to give, but he escapes.

Leon appears and orders Buwaya to return Tong's pearls and tells him to talk to Aso, but in return, he gives Tong two eggs to give to Manok and Pagong.
While heading there, he hears a meeting between the insects of the forest, led by Tipaklong, who plans a rebellion against the rest of the animals.

Tong meets Manok, and gives her an egg, which she accepts. He then meets Pagong, and is embarassed to hear that he gave the wrong eggs to each one. Nonetheless, they lead him to Aso.

He then meets Aso, who agrees to get the heart for him, but wants the heart's powers in return. However, Kuneho appears and objects, wanting its powers for himself. All the animals argue and thus they hold an election as to who should be crowned king. The election quickly fell into chaos, and thus, they settle it with a contest, which Langgam won.

Unfortunately, Langgam was accidentally stepped on and killed. The animals instead scrambled for the heart, climbing on top of one another to get it. They eventually did, but amidst the chaos, Leon and Buwaya appeared, as well as Tong's brother Ulang, wanting revenge for Tong's earlier actions. Chaos erupted as the three massacred the animals, while Tong fades out in the process.

Finally, Tong wakes up and meets Matsing, who helps him by giving him the heart. Matsing tells him the truth about the state of the forest, which inspires Tong. With a newfound resolve, Tong gains his confidence in standing up for his beliefs and bringing change to the forest.

Explanation

“It doesn't hurt to be good.”

On one scene, Leon takes advantage of Tong's situation. He tricks Tong through manipulation and even makes false promises to gain his trust and cooperation. This shows people with a higher position exploit or use other people's vulnerability for their own gain. They go to the point where they betray and give false hope because they are aware of one's weaknesses or are aware of their needs. It is important that one is aware of their influence and power and put it to good use and not just to gain more power and become cruel.

“Money can indeed buy anything, even against everyone's will.”

In the story, Tipaklong, Pagong, and Kuneho are fighting for the position of being the king of the forest. Due to ongoing bickering, Aso suggested holding an election so that the decision would be fair and systematic. However, the election was chaotic, with the animals unfairly stealing votes from each other. In the Philippines, this part of the story shows the issue of vote buying. People who are common victims of this kind of bribery are those who are less fortunate. This kind of act is unfair since it steals the title from people who are more deserving.

“Wake up! Look around you!”

The apathy of our society is metaphorically portrayed in the story. the animals have opted to show indifference amidst challenges around them, and is still seen in contemporary Filipino society. Most of the citizens are numb toward crucial social problems like poverty, unemployment, and inefficiency in the government. Values such as honesty, friendliness, and community spirit have lost much of their importance in contemporary society and have been replaced with the search for wealth, power, and personal success. There can be little doubt that ethics and social cohesion have suffered in this respect.

"When will men ever take no as an answer?"

In the story, Palaka wants to kiss Bibe because one day Palaka will become a handsome man, but Bibe doesn't want to because he kisses Palaka several times and becomes slimy, which is said to be his mouth. This mirrors harassment in the Philippines, which encompasses a range of behaviors that create a hostile environment, ften manifesting in workplaces, educational institutions, and public spaces. This issue is exacerbated by cultural norms that may discourage victims from speaking out, leading to underreporting and a lack of accountability for perpetrators. The Philippine government has enacted laws aimed at addressing harassment, yet societal attitudes and systemic barriers continue to hinder effective enforcement and support for those affected. As awareness grows, it is crucial to foster a culture of respect and accountability to combat harassment in all its forms.
